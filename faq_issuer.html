ISSUER FAQ

MOST FREQUENT QUESTIONS AND ANSWERS

  • What is Bambucorn?

    Bambucorn is an investment (equity) crowd funding platform will be regulated by the Dubai Financial Services Authority (“DFSA”) (as defined in the glossary). Bambucorn currently has an in-principle approval from the DFSA for an Innovation Testing License (“ITL”). Bambucorn is currently restricted under ITL to testing its platform and due to the limited nature of the ITL, normal client protections may not apply and clients may have limited rights if they suffer a loss as a result of taking part in testing.

  • What is Tokenization?

    In the context of Bambucorn, tokenization refers to the process of digitally representing an investment. When investors subscribe to a particular investment opportunity by signing the Investment Subscription Agreement (“ISA”) and transfer the amount, investors will be issued a token that represents their ownership of shares of the underlying company. By virtue of holding the token, investors will retain all economic, voting and other rights associated with the ownership of the underlying shares.

  • Why tokenization and not a normal issuance of shares?

    Tokenization will allow investors to sell their tokens on regulated Security Token Exchanges (STEs), thus increasing liquidity for their investments and providing a price-discovery mechanism. These benefits may likely result in a higher valuation for start-ups in subsequent rounds of fundraising.

  • Can start-ups on the platform issue normal uncertificated shares instead of tokens?

    No, all issuers must issue tokens. Issuance of tokens is akin to the issuance of shares. The only difference is that the issuance of tokens allows investors to realize the value of their investment much earlier. Issuers will need to ensure that their jurisdiction of incorporation allows for issuance of tokens.

  • What type of start-ups are listed on Bambucorn?

    Currently, Bambucorn lists start-ups that are looking for pre-seed or seed funding. To be listed on Bambucorn, start-ups must have at least developed a Minimum Viable Product (“MVP”) or prototype.

  • How do I get started?

    Founders are encouraged to apply via our application portal. If we believe that your start-up is what investors are looking for, a member of our team will contact you.

  • What are the benefits of being crowd funded?

    As a start-up, we understand that fundraising is a tedious process for start-ups. By presenting your offering on Bambucorn, investors will be able to review your offering at their own convenience, while you are the able to focus all your efforts on managing your start-up.

  • How much can I raise on the platform?

    Currently, Bambucorn has received an in-principle approval for an Innovation Testing License (“ITL”) from the DFSA which will not allow us to list offerings greater than USD 200K. However, upon our graduation from the Testing Phase, issuers listed on our platform will be allowed to raise up to USD 5 million in a calendar year.

  • What documents am I required to submit as part of the application process?

    As part of our due diligence process, we will require the following documents:

    • Certificate of Incorporation
    • Articles of Association (“AOA”) (optional)
    • Pitch-deck
    • Business plan/ Financial projections (optional)
    • Passport copy of founder(s)

    We may require additional documents as part of our due diligence process. A member of our team will reach out if this is the case.

  • How do I value my company?

    As part of the application process, you are asked to include desired terms of your offering. Our internal team will review your application and provide their inputs by accepting or proposing different terms for the proposed offering.

  • How much will I be charged for being listed on Bambucorn?

    You will pay only if your offering closes successfully. Fees that will be applied if your offering is successful are:

    • $0 retainer
    • Cash fee on funds raised through the platform. This fee will vary from issuer to issuer and will be discussed on a case by case basis
    • Any additional legal fees (if incurred)

  • Can my company be incorporated anywhere?

    Currently, we only list start-ups incorporated in the UAE. However, we have plans in the making to list start-ups from outside of the UAE upon graduation from the Testing Phase. Leave your email here to subscribe to updates in our listing policy.

  • What happens if my round is oversubscribed?

    The platform’s systems shall allow for oversubscriptions to all offerings listed on the platform. However, an issuer cannot collect proceeds that exceed the targeted amount of the offering. In the event, an offering is oversubscribed to, subscribers to the offering shall be allotted tokens on a pro-rata based on the amount(s) committed.

  • How long will the offering be open?

    The length of time an offering will be open will be issuer specific. However, we expect that offerings on the platform will be open up to a period of 90 days.

  • Is there any lock up period prior to investors trading tokens? Do we have a say in determining the lock up period?

    The lock up period will be issuer specific and will be specified in the terms of the offering.

  • What happens after my offering is over?

    After the offering is over, a decision is made on the status of the offering (success/ fail). If the offering is deemed a success, all money (net of fees) is transferred to your bank account. In return for the money, the issuer will issue tokens and the name(s) of the token holder(s) will be recorded on your register of shareholders. Once the token is listed on an exchange, you will be provided a capitalization table management solution to monitor the identity of the token holders. This cap table will be updated in real-time.
