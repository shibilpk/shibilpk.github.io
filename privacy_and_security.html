PRIVACY AND SECURITY POLICY

Bambucorn Ltd (‘Bambucorn’) will, upon fulfilling certain conditions, be a company incorporated in Dubai International Financial Centre (‘DIFC’). Bambucorn enables investors to invest in early-stage and growth focused businesses while getting experts to assist them in their due diligence process. Throughout this Privacy and Security Policy, Members, Issuers, Experts and other persons with whom Bambucorn interacts will be referred to as ‘you’ and Bambucorn will be referred to as ‘we’ or ‘us’.
This Privacy and Security Policy applies to the Bambucorn’s website and its sub-domains and related mobile applications, if any (collectively referred to as ‘Site’).

1. Security

All information provided to us by you or otherwise is stored on our technology partner’s secure servers. If we have given you (or you have chosen) a password or wallet number, you are responsible for keeping this password confidential. We ask you not to share these details with anyone and to inform us if you believe they have been compromised.

2. Choice

You can manage the kind of emails you receive from us by following the unsubscribe instructions in these emails or by accessing the email preferences in your [account settings] page. You can also alter your cookie preferences through the Site. It is important to note that disabling or making changes to these preferences may prevent you from using the full range of services available on the Site.

3. Accuracy and retention of Personal Data

If you see that any of your Personal Data included in the Site is inaccurate, please update it using the Site. Alternatively, if you believe that any Personal Data, we hold about you is inaccurate, please notify us at [the Data Protection team’s email address]. It is your responsibility to ensure that any Personal Data you have provided to us remains accurate and to notify us if there are any changes in your Personal Data. We will retain your Personal Data for as long as you use our Site and at least six years following that to comply with regulatory recordkeeping obligations we have.

4. Data Protection Principles

  1. Based on the Data Protection Law 2007, we will be classified as a ‘Data Controller’. This means that we will be responsible for determining the purposes and means of the Processing of Personal Data. Bambucorn will ensure that your Personal Data will be:
    1. Processed fairly, lawfully and securely;
    2. Processed for specified, explicit and legitimate purposes in accordance with your rights not further Processed in a way incompatible with those rights;
    3. Adequate, relevant and not excessive in relation to the purposes for which it is collected and/or further processed;
    4. Accurate and where necessary, kept up to date; and
    5. Kept in a form which permits your identification for no longer than is necessary for the purposes for which the Personal Data was collected or for which they are further Processed.

5. Information that we collect

  1. 1.1 Information you give us.
    1. When you use the Site, we may ask you to provide personal information such as contact details, identity documents, banking details, sources of funds and wealth, and in the case of Issuers, information about your business including but not limited to company name, company size, address, contact details, nature of business, ownership structure, financial information and forecasts and information about persons associated with the business. Most of this information is to comply with our legal obligations and to fulfil our contractual obligations with you and other parties that use the Site. In some cases, sharing the information is necessary for us to provide our services.
  3. 1.2 Information we get from your use of our services and by communicating with us.
    1. We collect information about the services you use and how you use them, such as when you view and interact with the Site or our advertisements. In addition, we may gather certain information about you, including details of your operating system, browser version, domain name and IP address using which you access the Site. We may also collect your Personal Data (any data relating to an identifiable natural person as defined in The Data Protection Law 2007). When you interact with our employees on the phone or in person, we may record those conversations, either because we are required to do so under regulation, or for training purposes so that we can improve our services.
  5. 1.3 Cookies.
    1. We may set and access cookies and other similar technologies on your computer. These are small files that may reside on your computer’s hard drive that are accessible only by the website that placed them there and not by any other website. If we have third-party advertisements on the Site, those third parties may also place, set, and access cookies on your computer. They do this to track how many unique users have seen an advertisement and to provide advertisements that are more relevant to a user’s interests. We do not have access to these cookies and, other than allowing them to be served, we play no role in these cookies at all. Cookies placed by us will allow us to track your browsing behaviour, links clicked and to collect various data, including analytics about how you use our Site. This will allow us to provide you with a better experience on our Site, and to collect, analyse and improve the performance of our services. You can choose to enable or disable cookies in your web browser. In addition, you may delete cookies that have already been placed on your hard drive.
  7. 1.4 Supplemented Data.
    1. We may collect information about you from other sources, including publicly available databases or third parties from whom we have purchased information, in which case we may combine this data with information we already have about you so that we can update, expand and analyse the accuracy of our records.

6. Your Rights

  1. You have the right to access any information we hold about you. You may ask us:
    1. To confirm in writing as to whether or not Personal Data relating to you is being Processed.
    2. To communicate to you in an intelligible form of the Personal Data undergoing Processing and of any available information as to its source.
    3. To rectify, erase or block Personal Data, the Processing of which does not comply with the provisions of the Data Protection Law 2007.
    4. To stop at any time on reasonable grounds, the Processing of Personal Data relating to yourself.
    5. To be informed before Personal Data is disclosed for the first time to third parties or used on their behalf for the purposes of direct marketing, and to be expressly offered the right to object to such disclosures or uses.
    6. To send you one copy of the Personal Data you have provided to us. To do so please contact us at [the Data Protection team’s email address]. This will generally be provided to you free of charge, but we may charge a reasonable fee if the request is unfounded, excessive, or repetitive.

7. Information that we may share

  1. Personal Data we collect will not be disclosed to any third parties by us or our affiliates except in the following circumstances:
    1. We may disclose your Personal Data to third party service providers solely in the course of their provision of services to Bambucorn and will take reasonable precautions to ensure that these service providers are obliged to keep your Personal Data secure and confidential, in compliance with relevant legal requirements. Your Personal Data may be transferred outside the United Arab Emirates (U.A.E.) and if this is the case, we will ensure that the transfer is protected by safeguards provided under U.A.E. law.
    2. We may disclose your Personal Data to fulfil any legal or regulatory obligation we may have.
    3. In the event that we intend to enter into a major corporate transaction, such as a sale of control of the Site, we may disclose certain of your Personal Data to potential buyers, underwriters or advisors. If we do this, we will take reasonable precautions to ensure that the recipients of your Personal Data are obliged to keep it secure and confidential, in compliance with relevant legal requirements.
    4. We may disclose a Member’s Personal Data to an Issuer if the Member has decided to invest in the Issuer via our Site.
    5. We may disclose your Personal Data to an Expert to facilitate communication between yourself and the Expert.
    6. We may disclose your Personal Data to third parties to conduct industry research. We will only do so if you have provided your consent for this purpose.

8. How we use your Information

We use the information we collect in the following manner:

3.1 Comply with legal and regulatory obligations:

  1. To confirm your identity to ensure you are eligible to use the services provided on the Site;
  2. To conduct required anti-money laundering checks in connection with the transactions you may conduct through the Site;
  3. To ensure you are eligible to use the Site in accordance with the Platform Agreements; and
  4. To enable us to comply with our compliance responsibilities and reporting to regulators and governmental authorities.

3.2 Carry out obligations under our contracts:

To process and administer payments and investments in connection with the services provided on our Site.

In the case of Issuers:

  1. To assess your suitability to execute a Campaign on the Site;
  2. To verify and approve the disclosures you make in your Campaign to potential investors; and
  3. To provide you with any Campaign marketing services and to facilitate the investment transactions.

In the case of Members:

  1. To facilitate any investments you make;
  2. To exercise any rights you may have by virtue of being a token holder; and
  3. To provide Issuers with information about the progress of their Campaigns and your investments in it.

In the case of Experts

  1. To share information about you with Members and Issuers.

In the case of Members who have completed investments in fundraising businesses:

  1. To provide those businesses with information to fulfil obligations to you and us and ensure compliance with their corporate or regulatory requirements, such as shareholder reporting.

3.3 Ensure that services provided by us through our Site operate effectively and to do this we may use your Personal Data:

  1. To monitor, improve and administer the Site and the services provided on the Site;
  2. To contact you to ensure customer satisfaction in respect of our role as a service provider and assist you in getting the best value from our services; and
  3. To respond to customer support or other enquiries requesting information.

We may also intercept, record and/or monitor any communication(s) (including telephone calls or other electronic communications) between you and us. We may do so in order to resolve queries in the future and for the purposes of ensuring security, staff training and complying with our regulatory and legal responsibilities. In the case of communications by email, you should note that as emails are not encrypted (and may therefore be intercepted by third parties) and as the identity of the sender cannot be confirmed, you must not provide your security details by email.

3.4 Facilitate communication between you and others, publicizing our business, support industry research, and making sure that marketing, advertising, and content we provide is effective. We will only contact you for this purpose if you have consented to do so.
